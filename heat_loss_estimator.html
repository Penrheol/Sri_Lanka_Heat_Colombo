Find your optimal warning threshold

This tool estimates the probability level at which acting on a heat warning becomes cost-effective for your sector — the core parameter of the Bayesian Heat Early Warning System.

How it works
📋
12 questions
About your sector, costs, and the consequences of inaction. About 5 minutes.
📐
C/L ratio
Your cost-to-loss ratio and optimal threshold p* are computed from your answers.
📥
Export results
Download your parameters as a JSON file and email it to the CCC coordinator.

Data collection: This tool is part of a research initiative under the South Asia Hydromet Forum to develop a Bayesian Heat Early Warning System for Sri Lanka. Your results are exported as a JSON file that you email to the CCC coordinator, contributing to a database of sectoral heat loss functions — real Sri Lankan data that will replace the generic benchmarks currently used to calibrate the system.

There is no right answer — this reflects how your institution weighs missed events against false alarms.
